##Introduction

Grammatical type: noun.   
Occurrences:   2x HB (0/2/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 2x).

* Nebiim: Joel 2:6; Nah 2:11. 

<b>A.1</b> In Isa 10:12 Spronk wishes to read <span dir="rtl">פארור</span> for <span 
dir="rtl">תפארת</span>, ‘glory, splendour’ (Spronk 1997:104). In that case it describes the colour of 
the Assyrian king’s haughty eyes (<span dir="rtl">רום עיניו</span>). In Spronk’s judgement <span 
dir="rtl">פארור</span> fits the syntagm better than <span dir="rtl">תפארת</span>. This 
reconstruction is not accepted due to the lack of textual and linguistic support.

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> In Scripture <span dir="rtl">פארור</span> occurs twice, both times in the idiom <span 
dir="rtl">קִבֵּץ פארור</span>, which has the sense of either ‘blush, become red’ or ‘grow pale’. In this 
phrase <span dir="rtl">פארור</span> references the ‘pinkish’ colour of human complexion.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 181-184 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
