##Introduction

Grammatical type: adjective.  
Occurrences:   3x HB (1/2/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 3x).

* Torah: Gen 25:25;
* Nebiim: 1 Sam 16:12; 17:42.

<b>A.1</b> In 1 Sam 17:42 McCarter deletes <span dir="rtl">ואדמני עם יפה מראה</span>, judging it to 
be a later addition drawn from the text in 1 Sam 16:12 (McCarter 1980:288-89). This position is not 
followed since the MT is supported by the versions. Nevertheless, there is evidence that during its 
transmission history the text of 1 Sam 17:42 has been influenced by the wording in 1 Sam 16:12 as is 
apparent in LXX’s reading πυρράκης μετὰ κάλλους ὀφταλμῶν in 1 Sam 17:42, wording that is in 
accord with the MT <span dir="rtl">אדמני עם יפה עינים</span> of 1 Sam 16:12 (<span 
dir="rtl"> יפה עינים</span> also appears in Gen 39:6). Vg appears to read <span 
dir="rtl">יפה מראה</span> in both references. The MT is preferred in both texts since it is the 
tendency for texts after being copied numerous times to conform to a singular reading.

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> <span dir="rtl">אדמוני</span> represents a ‘reddish-brown’ or ‘auburn’ colour; it also conveys the 
‘glowing’ quality of this colour. In its few occurrences this lexeme describes the colour of a person’s 
complexion and/or hair. Brenner proposes that <span dir="rtl">אדמדם</span> served as a synonym or 
a substitute for <span dir="rtl">אדמוני</span>; and by exilic times <span dir="rtl">אדמדם</span> had 
displaced <span dir="rtl">אדמוני</span> (Brenner 1982:128). That these terms are overlapping 
synonyms is supported by the fact that Tg<sup>PsJ</sup> glosses both <span dir="rtl">אדמדם</span>
in Lev 13:42 and <span dir="rtl">אדמוני</span> in Gen 25:25 with <span dir="rtl">סמקריי</span>.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 119-122 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
