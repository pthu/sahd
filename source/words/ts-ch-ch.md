##Introduction

Grammatical type: verb.   
Occurrences:   1x HB (0/0/1); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 1x).


* Ketubim: Lam 4:7.

<b>A.1</b> In the collocation <span dir="rtl">יָצָא מֵחֵלֶב עֵינֵמוֹ</span> (Ps 73:7), Gunkel proposes 
reading <span dir="rtl">יֵצַח</span>, ‘their eyes are milky white’, for MT <span dir="rtl">יָצָא</span>,
'swell' (Gunkel <sup>5</sup>1968:317). Without emending the text, Dahood arrives at a similar 
sense by arguing that <span dir="rtl">יצא</span> also means ‘be clean, shine, gleam’ (Dahood 
1968:189; cf. Dahood 1966:93): ‘their eyes gleam through folds of fat’ (REB). To achieve 
agreement between <span dir=”rtl”>ענימו</span> and <span dir="rtl">יָצָא</span>, Dahood repoints 
the verb as an inf.abs. <span dir="rtl">יָצֹא</span>. However, given the lack of textual support and the 
fact that MT is intelligible, both proposed emendations are rejected. 




## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> 
Glosses for <span dir="rtl">צחח</span> include ‘be bright, brilliant, glowing, radiant’. The roots <span 
dir="rtl">צחח</span> and <span dir="rtl">לבן</span> describe the glistening white colour of snow 
and milk. These lexemes stand for the same basic chromaticity, but they differ in regard to brilliance, 
lustre, and sheen. <span dir="rtl">לבן</span> references a light colour, ‘white to light grey’, and <span 
dir="rtl">צחח</span> the ‘radiance’ of a light colour (Brenner 1982:74).
