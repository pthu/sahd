## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> 
<span dir="rtl">קדרנית</span> is an adverb conveying ‘darkly, dully, dimly’. Its only attested OT 
occurrence is in the collocation <span dir="rtl">הלך קדרנית</span>. This idiom may refer to the 
custom of wearing dark, soiled clothing as an expression of sorrow, or it may be used metaphorically 
for the despondent demeanour of those sorely troubled by an oppressive situation. Conducting 
oneself in this solemn manner was a way of entreating God’s help; it was associated with piety, 
especially in the post-exilic community. The context is necessary to determine which sense is 
conveyed by this idiom.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 55-57 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
