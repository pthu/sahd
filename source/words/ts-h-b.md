## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> <span dir="rtl">צהב</span> may be glossed with ‘be bright, shine, polish, burnish’. It describes 
polished copper. Over time the lexeme came to denote the colour ‘yellow, reddish yellow’, possibly in 
LBHeb. When it gained this sense, <span dir="rtl">ירק</span>, which had covered the ‘yellow-green’ 
span, became limited to ‘green’. Besides serving as a colour lexeme in Rabbinic Heb., the root also 
conveys the sense of ‘mock, grieve, make angry’, possibly by metonymy.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 135-138 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
