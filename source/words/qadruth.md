##Introduction

Grammatical type: noun.  
Occurrences:   1x HB (0/1/0); 0x Sir; 1x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 2x).

* Nebiim: Isa 50:3;
* Qumran: 1QH 13.31.


<b>A.1</b> In Ezek 26:16 Dalman and <i>BHS</i> recommend emending MT <span dir="rtl">חרדות</span>,
‘trembling’, to <span dir="rtl">קדרות</span> (<i>ASP</i>, vol. 5, p. 214; accepted by Fohrer 1955:151, 
though he deletes v. 1aβ-b). <span dir="rtl">קדרות</span>, then, describes the princes of Tyre as 
putting on dark clothing to mourn the downfall of their magnificent city based on the view that a 
technical use of <span dir="rtl">קדרות</span> stands for the wearing of dark, unkempt clothing. 
Neither Cooke nor Zimmerli accept this emendation due to the lack of textual evidence and the fact 
that the collocation <span dir="rtl">לבשׁ שׁממה</span>, which is similar to <span 
dir="rtl">הלבישׁ חרדות</span>, is used metaphorically for utter despair in Ezek 7:27 (Cooke 1936:292; 
Zimmerli 1969:610).


## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> 
<span dir="rtl">קדרות</span> stands for ‘darkness’, particularly the darkness that comes over the 
earth when heavy, dark clouds fill the sky. It also references ‘dark clothing’ that is worn as an 
expression of mourning and despondency.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 53-55 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
