## 6. Exegesis

### 6.1 Textual Evidence


<b>A.1</b> This word occurs only once, in Ps. 56:9(8), <span dir="rtl">בְּסִפְרָתֶךָ</span> <span dir="rtl">הֲלׂא</span>, ‘Are they not in your book?’. The subject refers to the tears of the pious that God keeps with him in a waterskin. Word-play may be observed with <span dir="rtl">סָפַרְתָֹּה</span>, ‘you have counted’,  earlier in the verse. Sometimes the phrase <span dir="rtl">בְּסִפְרָתֶךָ</span> <span dir="rtl">הֲלׂא</span> is seen as a gloss, but the grounds for this hypothesis are insufficient (Zenger 2000, 108). Tur-Sinai 1957, 382-3, proposed to take <span dir="rtl">ספרה</span>  as the equivalent of <span dir="rtl">שפרה</span>, ‘bag, net’ in Job 26:13, but Clines 2006, 624, rightly remarks ‘It is not wise to follow Tur-Sinai’.

There is no 
explanation as to why the biblical author did not 
use the common masculine form → <span dir="rtl">סֵפֶר</span> here, but preferred the uncommon feminine. Perhaps he wanted to indicate that the ‘book’ was no real object but an abstract concept (<i>GBHrev</i>, § 134q-r). Some of the ancient versions also point in that direction (cf. section 5).  In this particular context <span dir="rtl">סִפְרָה</span>  refers to the <span dir="rtl">זִכָּרׂון</span> <span dir="rtl">סֵפֶר</span>,
‘Gedenkbuch’ (Book of Remembrance) in which  the YHWH records the names and deeds of pious sufferers (Mal 3:16; cf. Kraus 1960, 409; Hill 1998, 339-40). This book is probably the same as the <span dir="rtl">חַיִּים</span> <span dir="rtl">סֵפֶר</span>, ‘Book of Life’, mentioned in Ps 69:29(28). See also Exod 32:32; Ps 40:8(7). According to apocalyptic literature it will be opened at the Final Judgment (Dan 7:10; 12:4; TLevi 1:18; Asen. 15:4; Rev 20:12, etc.). The concept of a heavenly book has very ancient roots (see e.g. Widengren 1950; Paul 1973).



It is unclear whether the use in Ps 56:9(8) should be regarded as figurative or not.

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> <span dir="rtl">סִפְרָה</span> denotes a special kind of book, to wit: the book in which God takes notes 
of all the evil and suffering the faithful righteous
encounter in their lives. It occurs only once in 
the Hebrew Bible.
