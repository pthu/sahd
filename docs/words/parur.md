## 4. Ancient Versions

<b>Septuagint (LXX)</b><sup id="fnref:1"><a href="#footnote" data-toggle="modal" onclick="show_modal('fn:1')">1</a></sup> 

[^1]: The translations of the Greek are based on <i>GELS</i>.

* χύτρα, ‘earthen pot’: Num 11:8; Judg 6:19; Sir 13:2;
* χύτρα, or κύθρα as variant of χύτρα, ‘earthen pot’: 1 Sam 2:14 (see A.1).

<b>A.1</b> 
Most of the manuscripts of the LXX represent the four Hebrew terms for cooking vessels in the MT of 1Sam 2:14 – 
<span dir="rtl">כִּיּוֹר</span>,
<span dir="rtl">דּוּד</span>,
<span dir="rtl">קַּלַּחַת</span>, 
<span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span>
– by only three Greek expressions: 
λέβης + μέγας – χαλκεῖον  – χύτρα/κύθρα. 
These three Greek renderings occur in this order in Codex Vaticanus LXX<sup><small>B</small></sup>: 
καὶ ἐπάταξεν αὐτὴν εἰς τὸν λέβητα τὸν μέγαν ἢ εἰς τὸ χαλκίον ἢ εἰς τὴν κύθραν, ‘and he thrusted it into the large cauldron and into the bronze cauldron and into the earthen pot’. The deviating order
λέβης + μέγας - χύτρα - χαλκεῖον  occurs
in LXX<sup><small>Ant</small></sup>.<sup id="fnref:2"><a href="#footnote" data-toggle="modal" onclick="show_modal('fn:2')">2</a></sup>
Elsewhere in the LXX, both the occurrences of <span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span> (and also 
<span dir="rtl">פָּארוּר</span> in Joel 2:6; Nah 2:11) 
and the other occurrence of <span dir="rtl">קַלַּחַת</span> (Mic 3:3)
are translated as χύτρα/κύθρα. Only in 1 Sam 2:14 do the Hebrew nouns
<span dir="rtl">קַלַּחַת</span> and <span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span> occur together. It is quite likely that in the Greek translation of 1 Sam 2:14 χύτρα/κύθρα represents both <span dir="rtl">קַלַּחַת</span> and <span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span>.

[^2]: LXX<sup><small>Ant</small></sup>: εἰς τὸν λέβητα τὸν μέγαν ἢ εἰς τὴν χύθραν ἢ εἰς τὸ χαλκεῖον (Fernández Marcos & Busto Saiz 1989:8).

<b>Targum (Tg)</b>
 
* <span dir="rtl">מָלֵיסָא</span>, ‘cooking vessel’:
1 Sam 2:14;  
* <span dir="rtl">קדרא</span>, ‘pot’:
Num 11:8 (Tg<sup><small>O</small></sup>); Judg 6:19. 

<b>A.1</b>
In the targumim <span dir="rtl">מָלֵיסָא</span>, ‘cooking vessel’, is a very rare word, which occurs only in the translation of 1 Sam 2:14 and 2 Chron 35:13. In 1 Sam 2:14 it was probably chosen to represent <span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span> because <span dir="rtl">קדרא</span> already represented the preceding term <span dir="rtl">קַלַּחַת</span>. 



<b>Vulgate (Vg)</b><sup id="fnref:4"><a href="#footnote" data-toggle="modal" onclick="show_modal('fn:4')">4</a></sup> 

[^4]: The translations of the Latin are based on <i>OLD</i>. 


* <i>olla</i>, ‘pot’, ‘jar’: Num 11:8; Judg 6:19;   
* <i>caccabus</i> (< κάκκαβος), ‘cooking-pot’: 1 Sam 2:14; Sir 13:2 (Vg 13:3).


<b>A.1</b>
In the Vulgate <i>caccabus</i> is a very rare word, which occurs only in the translation of 1 Sam 2:14, 2 Chron 35:13 and Sir 13:2 (Vg 13:3). In 1 Sam 2:14 is was probably chosen to represent
<span dir="rtl">פָּרוּר</span> 
because <i>olla</i> already represented the preceding term <span dir="rtl">קַלַּחַת</span>. In Sir 13:2/3 it was probably chosen so that <i>olla</i> could be used to represent the following word <span dir="rtl">סִיר</span>.
