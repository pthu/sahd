##Introduction

Grammatical type: verb.   
Occurrences:   2x HB (0/2/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 2x)

* Nebiim: Isa 19:9 (1QIsa<sup>a</sup>; see entry <span dir="rtl">חורי</span>); 29:22.

<b>A.1</b> In Isa 19:9 in place of MT <span dir="rtl">1 חוֹרָֽי</span>QIsa<sup>a</sup> reads <span 
dir="rtl">חורו</span>. Unfortunately the text of 4QIsa<sup>c</sup> (4Q163 11.2.1) is fragmented 
here. KB<sup>3</sup> points 1QIsa<sup>a</sup> <span dir="rtl">חָוֵֽרוּ</span> (‘erbleichen’, 
<i>i.e.</i>, ‘they have grown pale’; KB<sup>3</sup>, p. 287a). Kaiser, Clements, and Wildberger 
accept this reading (Kaiser 1974:98; Wildberger 1978:701; Clements 1980:168). Even prior to the 
discovery of 1QIsa<sup>a</sup> several scholars, including Marti and Gray (Marti 1900:153; G.B. 
Gray 1912:326-28), proposed that the Heb. should read <span dir="rtl">חורו</span>. <i>DCH</i> 
offers the pointing <span dir="rtl">יֶחֱוָרוּ</span>, ‘they will become white, pale’ (<i>DCH</i>, vol. 3, p. 178b). On the other hand, the glosses in the LXX, βύσσον, and the Vg, <i>subtilia</i>, indicate that 
these translators read the Heb. as a noun, likely <span dir="rtl">חורי</span> from the root <span 
dir="rtl">חור</span>, <i>i.e.</i>, ‘a finely woven white fabric’. Several commentators as Oswalt and 
Watts follow the MT as offering an intelligible reading (Oswalt 1986:365, on the basis of the LXX; 
Watts 2005:307 n. 19.c). 

<b>A.2</b> In Isa 24:6 1QIsa<sup>a</sup> reads <span dir="rtl">חורו</span>, ‘they are pale’, for MT 
<span dir="rtl">חָרוּ</span>, ‘they are scorched’ (from <span dir="rtl">חרר</span>, ‘be scorched, 
glow’; <i>DCH</i>, vol. 3, p. 322b). According to Wildberger, the sense conveyed by the verb in 
1QIsa<sup>a</sup> is too weak for this context (Wildberger 1978:914-15). He takes it from II <span 
dir="rtl">חרה</span>, cognate of Arab. ‘abnehmen’ and gives the gloss ‘sie schwinden dahin’. 
Another way to gain a solution for the MT is to find a root that parallels <span dir="rtl">נשׁאר</span>. 
KB<sup>3</sup> sets forth two proposals: II <span dir="rtl">חוּר</span> cognate of Arab. <i>ḥwr</i>
(‘weniger werden’; KB<sup>3</sup>, p. 287a) or II <span dir="rtl">חָרָה</span> ‘schwinden, an Zahl 
gering werden’, cognate of Arab. <i>ḥarāy</i> (‘abnehmen’; KB<sup>3</sup>, p. 337b). Wildberger, 
followed by Watts, accepts the latter possibility and offers the gloss ‘sie schwinden dahin’ (Wildberger 
1978:914-15; Watts 2005:374-75; <i>cf.</i> G.B. Gray 1912:411). Without strong textual support, it is 
best to follow the MT.


## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> 
<span dir="rtl">חור</span>, ‘be white, whiten’, is a verbal form of a Sem. root that was available to 
early speakers of Heb. It was displaced by the root <span dir="rtl">לבן</span> in Early BHeb. It 
reappears in LBHeb., possibly under the influence of Aram. In the two occurrences of this lexeme in 
BHeb., it is used metaphorically for deep despair. In Isa 29:22, <span dir="rtl">פנה חור</span> means 
literally ‘a face turns pale’. In the emended text of Isa 19:9 the verb is used with the same significance 
of describing deep shame, but without <span dir="rtl">פנה</span>.
Note: also consider the entries <span dir="rtl">לָבַן ,לָבָן</span>, and 
<span dir="rtl">לֹבֶן</span> for the colour ‘white’ in Heb.
