##Introduction

Grammatical type: adjective.   
Occurrences:   4x HB (2/2/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 4x).

* Torah: Gen 31:10, 12;
* Nebiim: Zech 6:3, 6.

<b>A.1</b> In Neh 5:18 for MT <span dir="rtl">בְּרֻרוֺת</span>, ‘choice’, a Qal part. pass. (also 1 Chron 
7:40; 9:22), a few mss read <span dir="rtl">בְּרֻדּוֺת</span> (BHS). Joüon, however, proposes reading 
<span dir="rtl">בריות</span> (Joüon 1931:87-88), a defective spelling of <span 
dir="rtl">בריאות</span> ‘fat’, with support from Syr. <i>šammināṯā’</i> ‘fat’. It is better to follow the 
MT for its reading offers sufficient sense (Williamson 1985:234 n. 18b).

<b>A.2</b> McHardy wishes to delete <span dir="rtl">ברדים</span> in Zech 6:3, speculating that it 
was a gloss introduced either under the influence of Gen 31:10, 12 or as an attempt to provide a true 
equine colour for the unknown <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> (McHardy 1968:176). The textual 
tradition, however, loans no support to the deletion of <span dir="rtl">ׁברדים</span> in this text.

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> <span dir="rtl">ברד</span> denotes ‘speckles, spots, or patches’. In the extant examples it identifies 
animals, specifically sheep or horses having these patterns. Most likely the spots, specks, or patches 
are ‘white’ or ‘light’ in colour. ‘Piebald’ offers a sound English gloss for animals.
