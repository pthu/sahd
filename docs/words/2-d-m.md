##Text doubtful

<b>B.1</b>
In Isa 63:1 <i>BHS</i> recommends emending
<span dir="rtl">מֵאֱדוֹם</span>
in the collocation
<span dir="rtl">בָּא מֵאֱדוֹם חֲמוּץ בְּגָדִים</span>, 
‘one comes from Edom in red stained garments’, to
<span dir="rtl">מְאָדָּם</span>,
‘reddened’.
This proposed change is not compelling, because it lacks textual support and because 
‘from Edom’ is paralleled in the second line by
מִבָּצְרָה, ‘from Bozrah’, a city in Edom—thus ‘Edom’ identifies the location of Bozrah (Gradwohl 1963:11). Another obstacle to the proposed emendation is the suggestion of its supporters to change the toponym 
<span dir="rtl">בצרה</span>, 
‘Bozrah’, to <span dir="rtl">בֹּצֵר</span>, <i>qal</i> part., ‘a grape picker’.
Koole (2001:331-32) judges the resulting phrase ‘more than a grape picker’ to offer little sense in this context, especially since a worker’s clothing becomes stained from treading grapes, not from picking them. Since the latter emendation has persuaded few contemporary scholars, the case for the initial emendation is greatly weakened. Furthermore, in the present text there is a play on the sound of
<span dir="rtl">אֱדוֹם</span>, 
‘Edom’,
and
<span dir="rtl">אָדוֹם</span>,
‘red’ (Whybray 1975:253), providing additional support for the MT reading.




##<span id="Con">7. Conclusion</span>

<span dir="rtl">אדם</span> in the <i>qal</i> references a range of colours, ‘orange’, ‘brown’, ‘reddish brown’, ‘red’, and even ‘bright red’. In reference to humans, it describes the ‘copper’ colour of a healthy complexion. After rams’ skins and shields were tanned, they appeared ‘reddish-brown’. In fact, the Heb. lexeme <span dir="rtl">מאדם</span> functions as a technical term for ‘tanned’ (King and Stager, <i>LBI</i>, 162-63). Metaphorically human sins are described as ‘red’; to ensure that <span dir="rtl">אדם</span>, ‘red’, in this collocation is understood as referencing a ‘bright red’ the comparative phrase <span dir="rtl">כתולע</span>, ‘like scarlet’, is added (Isa 1:18). This comparison indicates that when <span dir="rtl">אדם</span> references bright red, it is usually not as bright as scarlet. <span dir="rtl">אדם</span> also stands for ‘deep burgundy’ colour of wine. In the <i>pual</i>, <i>hiph.</i>, and <i>hithp.</i> <span dir="rtl">אדם</span> describes something ‘being made brown, reddish brown, or red’.
