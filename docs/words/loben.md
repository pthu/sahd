##Introduction

Grammatical type: noun masculine.<sup id="fnref:1"><a href="#footnote" data-toggle="modal" onclick="show_modal('fn:1')">1</a></sup>   
Occurrences:   0x HB; 1x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 1x).

* Sira: 43:18<sup><small>Mas</small></sup>

 
<b>A.1</b> In 43:18 Sir<sup><small>B</small></sup> reads <span dir="rtl">תואר לבנה</span>; <span dir="rtl">לבנה</span> is a 
substantivised fem. adj. Sir<sup><small>Mas</small></sup> reads <span dir="rtl"> תור לבנו</span>; <span dir="rtl">לבן</span> is a noun. 
Yadin, however, interprets <span dir="rtl">לבנה</span> as equalling <span dir="rtl">לבנֹה</span> 
(Yadin 1965:32a).

[^1]: <i>DCH</i>, vol. 4, p. 515a. This position is supported by LXX’s gloss λευκότης. KB<sup>3</sup> lists it under the adj. <span dir="rtl">לבן</span>.

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> <span dir="rtl">לֹבֶן</span> is a noun for ‘whiteness’ as is evident in its serving as a metonymy for 
‘snowflakes’.

<b>For this entry, see further John E. Hartley, <i>The Semantics of Ancient Hebrew Colour Lexemes</i> (2010), 96-97 (<a href="/store/printed_publications/">Printed publications</a>).</b>
