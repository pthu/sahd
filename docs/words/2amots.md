##Introduction

Grammatical type: adjective.   
Occurrences:   2x HB (0/2/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 2x).

* Nebiim: Zech 6:3, 7.

<b>A.1</b> Some interpreters like Brenner view <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> as a colour lexeme in 
Zech 6:3, 7, possibly referencing ‘reddish-white’ dust colour (Brenner 1982:113-14), while others as 
Haupt, Horst, and Rudolph delete this term from Zech 6:3, since the collocation <span 
dir="rtl">ברדים אמצים</span> is the only case of two asyndetic adjectives standing together as 
modifiers of horses in Zechariah’s first and eighth visions (1:8; 6:2-3, 6-8) (Haupt 1913:108-9 n. 6; 
Horst 1954:234; Rudolph 1976:122 n. 3a). Chary likewise deletes it, considering it to be a gloss 
inserted to specify the sense of <span dir="rtl">ברדים</span> (Chary 1969:106). Brenner (1982:113-14), 
however, keeps <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span>, glossing it as ‘light reddish brown’, and deletes <span 
dir="rtl">ברדים</span> in order to have a text containing a series of four solid colour lexemes, a text 
that corresponds to the vision of four horses of differing solid colours in the vision report in Rev 6:2-7, 
an account that is dependent on Zech. McHardy 1968:176 also takes this position). Nevertheless, its 
deletion lacks textual support except for the Pesh. Since the primary sense of the root <span 
dir="rtl">אמץ</span> ‘strong’ fits this context sufficiently well, the MT is followed.

<b>A.2</b> Brenner views <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> to be a secondary addition in Zech 6:7, 
postulating that <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> and <span dir="rtl">ברדים</span> were added by an 
editor after an original <span dir="rtl">אדמים</span> had dropped out of Zech 6:6 (Brenner 
1982:113). A better alternative finds that <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> is original in Zech 6:7, having 
the sense of ‘strong ones, steeds’ (Myers and Myers 1987:326; Chary 1969:106, 108; Hanhart 
1998:386-87; Willi-Plein 2007:112-13) (cf. 6.A.1 below).

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> The only two existing collocations in which <span dir="rtl">אמץ</span> may function as a possible 
colour lexeme in ancient Heb. occur in Zechariah’s eighth vision (6:3, 7). In that case <span 
dir="rtl">אמץ</span> is a colour lexeme used for identifying the team of horses drawing one of the 
chariots as ‘chestnut’ (Brenner 1982:114). However, it is possible to achieve a sound reading in both 
texts by exegeting <span dir="rtl">אמצים</span> as a form of the well-attested root <span 
dir="rtl">אמץ</span>, ‘strong ones’. In that case one does not need to postulate the existence of 
<span dir="rtl">אמץ</span> as a homonym to that root with the sense ‘red’ and, thus, as an 
overlapping synonym of <span dir="rtl">אדם</span>.
