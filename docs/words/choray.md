##Introduction

Grammatical type: noun feminine.   
Occurrences:   1x HB (0/1/0); 0x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 1x)

* Nebiim: Isa 19:9.

<b>A.1</b> In Isa 19:9 for MT <span dir="rtl">חוֺרָֽי</span> 1QIsa<sup>a</sup> reads <span 
dir="rtl">חורו</span> and BHS suggests <span dir="rtl">יֶחֱוָֽרוּ</span>. That <span 
dir="rtl">חורי</span> is a noun finds support in the glosses of LXX βύσσον and Vg <i>subtilia</i>, ‘finely 
woven white fabric’, and in its being parallel to <span dir="rtl">פשׁתים</span>, ‘linen’. Nevertheless, 
the reading of 1QIsa<sup>a</sup> has strong support; see ‘Introduction A.1’ under I <span 
dir="rtl">חור</span> verb.


## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> If Heb. has the lexeme <span dir="rtl">חוֹרַי</span>, it stands for ‘white fabric’, particularly ‘fine white 
linen’ for which Egypt was famous.
