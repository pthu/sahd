##Introduction

Grammatical type: verb.   
Occurrences:   3x HB (0/0/3); 1x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 4x).
 
* Ketubim: Job 16:16; Lam 1:20; 2:11;
* Sira: 4.2/3
* Qere/Ketiv: For Job 16:16 there is a K <span dir="rtl">פני חמרמרה</span> and a Q <span 
dir="rtl">פני הֳמַרְמְרוּ</span>. Dhorme takes the Q as a correction of the K since the subject is pl. 
(Dhorme 1967:238). In support of the K he cites the rule that a verb having a 3 m.pl. subject that is 
the name of a thing may stand in the 3 f.sg. (GKC, §145k). Gordis, however, interprets the K to be an 
old 3 f.pl. pf., a form that still exists in Arab. <i>qatalā</i> and in certain dialects of Aram.
<i>qetala</i> (Gordis 1978:177-78). In that case the K takes <span dir="rtl">פָּנִים</span> as f.; a 
similar example is found in Rabbinic Heb. (<i>e.g.</i>, m’Abot 1.15: <span 
dir="rtl">בסבר פנים יפות</span>). But GKC states that this latter position lacks sufficient support (GKC, 
§44m).

## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> An explanation that satisfactorily accounts for the different nuances conveyed by <span 
dir="rtl">חמרמר</span> in its three occurrences plus the singular occurrence of <span 
dir="rtl">החמיר</span> in Sir 4.2 has not been attained. It is possible that different roots, namely III
<span dir="rtl">חמר</span>, ‘boil, burn, glow’, and II <span dir="rtl">חמר</span>, ‘foam, ferment’, the 
root for <span dir="rtl">חֶמֶר</span>, ‘wine’, are attested. Lexemes from the latter root may allude to 
either the process of fermentation or the colour of wine, according to Brenner (1982:132). However, 
in its three occurrences <span dir="rtl">חמרמר</span> primarily describes painful conditions caused 
by irritations in areas of the body, namely the face or the bowels. Thus III <span dir="rtl">חמר</span> 
is not a colour lexeme.
