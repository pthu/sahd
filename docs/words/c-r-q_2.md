##Introduction

Grammatical type: verb.   
Occurrences:   0x HB; 2x Sir; 0x Qum; 0x Inscr.  (Total: 2x).

* Sira: 43.9<sup><small>Bmg,Mas</small></sup>; 50.7<sup><small>B</small></sup>



<b>A.1</b> For <span dir="rtl"> ואורו מזהיר במרומי אל</span> (Sir 43.9b<sup><small>B</small></sup>) Mas reads <span 
dir="rtl">עד ומשריק</span> … <span dir="rtl">במרו</span> and <sup><small>Bmg</small></sup> has <span 
dir="rtl"> ועדי משריק במרומי אל</span>. Yadin interprets <span dir="rtl">ׁעד</span> in Mas as ‘adorns’ 
and takes … <span dir="rtl">במרו</span>, ‘the hei[ghts of God]’, as obj. of ‘adorn’; he glosses <span 
dir="rtl">ומשריק</span>, ‘and sparkles therein’ (Yadin 1965:30-31, 47). Skehan and Di Lella emend Mas 
to read <span dir="rtl">עדי משריק</span> on the basis of <sup><small>Bmg</small></sup> (Skehan and Di Lella 1987:489 n.9b). 
The latter reading is followed for <span dir="rtl">עדי משריק</span> is a proper parallel to the 
corresponding phrases <span dir="rtl">כלי צבא</span> and <span dir="rtl">נבלי מרום</span> in Sir 
43.8c<sup><small>M</small></sup>.


## 7. Conclusion

<b>A.1</b> In the attested occurrences <span dir="rtl">שׂרק</span> functions as a part. modifying the sun and 
the morning star as ‘shining brightly’ in the heavens. <span dir="rtl">שׂרק</span> references 
‘brightness’ more than hue, as for hue there is a hint of a yellowish or golden colour as attested in the 
Sem. cognates.
